Writing
  A lot of people think you have to be a good writer to write. But, the truth is, as Langston Hughes once said, “The prerequisite to writing is having something to say".  


Whether you want to hone your skills in writing courses, writing groups, or just write alone, it can be a very meditative experience. Some ideas for what to write about include: personal stories, fictional stories, poetry, song lyrics, etc.
   
Reading
 
Reading allows me to temporarily escape into a different world, and it doesn’t cost much, if anything at all. If I have access to a library, I can get books for free. I can also consider using an e-book and purchasing books for a lower cost than some hardcover books may cost. I can also read online in PDF form, share books with friends, or even start a book club where I can exchange books.
   
Photography
 
Thanks to technology, most people have access to cameras on their phones. If I don’t want to splurge on buying a professional camera, I can hone my skills on my mobile phone instead.  


Additionally, there are apps and resources where I learn photo editing on either a computer or a phone.
